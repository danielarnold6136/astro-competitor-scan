  2. The ±2h hedging rule is arithmetically self-defeating as written. S30's approximate-time copy offers two candidate Rising signs ("Scorpio Rising — or Sagittarius, if you were born after 04:51").   4.   • Store (naive local datetime, IANA name, lat, lon). NEVER a UTC offset column. The browser never computes an offset; the IANA name goes to the ephemeris and the engine resolves the offset. Astroline ships the production version of this bug — "utc_offset":0 beside "time_zone":"America/New_York" — and passing −06:00 instead of −05:00 for a 15-June Chicago birth put the Sun 2′23″ wrong, exactly one hour of solar motion.
  • Geocoding is GeoNames cities500 (~185k rows, CC BY 4.0) in Postgres with a pg_trgm GIN index. Column 18 of the dump IS the IANA timezone. $0 forever, p95 <30 ms, no third-party call ever. Google Places + Time Zone is $10 per 1,000 leads ≈ $900/mo at 100k.
  • Never derive birth data from an IP. Astroline's skip path reverse-geocodes the visitor's IP into birth_place at 13 decimal places and shows it back as fact.
  • Require an explicit dropdown selection. Lumius's autocomplete handed our own walkers "Nova Praha, Ukraine" for Prague and "Athens, Canada" for Athens — both silently geocoded, both stored, both wrong by thousands of kilometres and a whole time zone.
  • Never partial-lead capture. SkyWhisper and Psychic Marie harvest the email field as it is typed (Marie polls every 400 ms); Flash Voyance creates a lead on window.onload before any interaction. "The single most indefensible thing in a regulator's eyes."
  • Never buy an email-validation API at launch. Nebula's returns 422 for gmail, outlook, hotmail, proton, icloud, gmx, yandex and zoho — every false positive is a paid-for lead binned.
  • Questions we will not ask, at any length: health or mental health (Meta Personal Attributes + GDPR Art. 9 + WA MHMDA, which has a private right of action), ethnicity preference, and competitor disparagement (CAP 3.41 — truth is no defence). Enforced by the banned-term build test, not by review.

10.6 The moat, restated as a build dependency

Of 28 brands and 2,315 ads, exactly one competitor has a provably correct ephemeris — astrofame.com, whose free rising-sign calculator matched an independent Meeus computation on 4 of 4 controlled cases, and which uses it as a lead magnet, not as a subscription funnel. Everyone else ships chart-shaped UI over unverified maths: SkyWhisper sends birth_date only while its paywall claims to decode "planetary positions, houses, and aspects" · tryastro never asks a time or a place and ships 12 static PNGs · hint.app geocodes but never asks a time, then claims "NASA data" · Astroline writes the visitor's IP city into birth_place at 13 decimal places and shows it back as fact · spiritualsociety.co hard-codes value="01/01/2000" for every buyer · Lumius is likely real but no output has ever been observed.

That is the moat, and it is only a moat if three things are built: the fixture suite (D3), the validator (D4), and the per-user computation log (D4). Without them, "we compute your real natal chart" is a claim we cannot substantiate — which puts us in the same paragraph as the operators this teardown exists to avoid.
