2.5 What the market has converged on (VERIFIED structural constants)

  1. The entry price is ~$1, everywhere, and it is not revenue. $1.00 (astroline, hint, tryastro), $0.99 (SkyWhisper, Aspectica), $4.99 (Moonly), $0 (Gaia). At US Stripe rates a $1.00 trial costs $0.33 to process — 32.9% of gross. It is a card-capture and intent filter.
  2. The cheap-looking tile is the expensive plan. Astroline's two "cheap" tiles rebill at $43.43/mo equivalent; the tile badged "SAVE 50%" is the only one at $29.99/mo — and that badge is arithmetically false (real: 30.8%).
  3. **Trial price buys trial length, not product.** Astroline: $1 → 7 days, $5.49 → 14 days, $9.99 → 30 days. The only mechanic in the set that turns the trial into real day-0 revenue.
  4. Nobody bids on the trial price. Astroline feeds Meta a predicted ARPAS, not the $1 (§9.5).

D1 — One question per screen; auto-advance on single-select. No Continue button except on wheels, multi-selects and text inputs. Saves ~16 taps.

D2 — A working Back button on every screen. Lumius renders ← Back on every screen 01–42 (verified across all 46 extracted DOMs). Astroline renders none.

D4 — Every skip states its cost, and Continue is never the gated path.

⭐⭐ The clearest artefact of intent in the whole repo: Astroline's birthday screen ships <button data-variant="link_brand">Skip</button> next to <button data-variant="fill_brand" disabled="">Continue</button>. The accurate path is disabled; the fabricated one is one tap. They log both skip events.

"We'll use a solar chart. Your reading will be complete, but your Rising sign and house placements will be marked as estimated — you can add your exact time later and we'll regenerate it free."

That last clause is not a compliance concession; it is a retention hook and an email re-engagement trigger.

D5 — Never write IP-derived data into a birth field. If birthplace is skipped, lat/lng = null and the reading says so. (Astroline reverse-geocodes the proxy exit IP into birth_place at 13 decimal places and displays it back as fact.)

5.2 ⚠️ The adversarial finding, stated plainly (verify_1)

The claim "the $1 → $14.99/wk → $39.99/mo ladder is viable" does not survive as stated. Severity: serious, not fatal. Four specific failures:

  1. "Viable" is not established, and this repo says so about itself. Viability is LTV > CAC + COGS. We have a modelled LTV, an out-of-scope COGS, and zero CAC. study_ad-ops.md: "We have ZERO cost, CPM, CPA or ban-rate data on any competitor… No CPA, CTR or ban-probability number appears in this document" and "We have no CPA for this vertical and I will not invent one."
  2. The $14.99/week rung has one source, and it is the weakest advertiser in the corpus. SkyWhisper, 118 lifetime ads. 17,800 ÷ 118 = 150.8× astroline; 5,460 ÷ 118 = 46.3× aspectica. The "tight clearing band $14.19–$16.03" is n = 1, read out of a shipped config file — merchant intent, not evidence that a single customer paid it.
  3. Nobody serves weekly and monthly side by side. Across 28 brands and 2,315 ads, not one operator does. The only mixed-cadence ladder is Astroline's, and it works only because it never prints the monthly-equivalent. Bob's spread (+62.4%) is the widest ever observed — 1.6× Nebula's, 1.4× Astroline's. The wider the spread, the more concealment the weekly tile requires.
  4. The conversion assumption is ~2× the only close benchmark. Planning at 20% trial→paid against RevenueCat's median Day-35 hard-paywall trial-to-paid of 10.7%. At 10.7% the ladder returns $27.85 per trial-start (−41.1%) against an unknown cost. Honest counterweight: card-required, opt-out trials in B2B SaaS are quoted at 35–55% (median ~44%), so the defensible range is 10.7%–44% and nothing narrows it.

Per-dispute economics are worse on weekly: a disputed $14.99 charge costs $29.99 and the $15 fee is 50% of the loss; a disputed $39.99 costs $54.99 with the fee at 27%; a disputed $1.00 trial costs $16.00 — the fee is 94% of it.

5.7 Refunds are a pricing instrument, not a concession

Refunds are strictly cheaper than chargebacks ($14.99 vs $29.99; $39.99 vs $54.99) and they do not touch the VAMP counter.

Our policy: a 30-day, self-serve, no-questions FULL refund on the first rebill, honoured automatically, with one number generated from one constant into five surfaces — paywall · card sheet · receipt · /refund · ToS.

Two rules straight from Stripe's own documentation:

  1. "Customers can't dispute fully refunded payments, [but] they can still dispute partially refunded payments… for the full payment amount" ⇒ NEVER partial-refund.
  2. "If you have a checkbox… that only contains a link, the issuer might reject it… There must be reasonable evidence that you presented your customer with a full copy of your policies" ⇒ render refund + renewal terms INLINE on the card sheet, not linked.

The corpus's most common defect is contradictory refund promises: wildlyrooted has four, Marie has six, tryastro/skywhisper say 30 on the paywall and 7 in /terms, and Astroline advertises "don't notice progress → complete refund" while its binding policy excludes "you dislike the product" and "you did not expect… the trial would convert."

5.9 What we recommend against

  1. Do not ship the two plans as a plain weekly-vs-monthly toggle. Either it is honest and the weekly tile is dead weight, or it is a decoy and it is exactly the pattern Stripe names.
  2. Do not ship a flat $1 trial (§5.5).
  3. Do not copy any competitor "SAVE X%" badge. Every one checked is arithmetically false. Compute ours at build time from the actual prices so it cannot drift.
  4. Do not use weekly in UK/IE beyond the first test cycle (§5.8).
  5. Do not put an annual plan on the acquisition paywall. Only Gaia does it, and Gaia is a media subscription with a brand. An annual cannibalises the $780/yr cohort to $140–$360/yr and pulls forward refund risk.
  6. Do not run a "cost to provide this" line unless the figure is real and substantiable (FTC ¶117 quotes Nebula's "the actual cost to us is $13.67" as part of the deception narrative).
  7. Do not send the trial price to Meta as the conversion value. Send a modelled first-90-day value — otherwise the optimiser buys $1 customers and the ladder never sees the traffic it was priced for.
  8. Do not run a per-minute or credit layer. Nebula's credit layer requires hiding the dollar rate to work (40 credits/min in-app vs 30 in the FAQ vs $3.99/min in marketing) — **the opacity is the mechanic.**

7.3 The design contract — seven rules enforced in the scheduler, not in a doc

  1. A global send budget: max 1 marketing email per person per day, 5 per week, counted across every stream. Never two within 6 hours. Transactional is exempt but capped by its own logic.
  2. Every promise about frequency is kept by code. If we say "one email a week from now on", a row is written to the preference record and the scheduler physically cannot exceed it.
  3. De-escalation is automatic, not requested. Engagement decays → frequency decays.
  4. Nothing is sent that the user did not consent to receive, with the consent record stored and quotable.
  5. Complaint rate is a circuit breaker, not a KPI. >0.10% pauses marketing automatically.
  6. Flows cannot collide. One central dispatcher checks the cap, quiet hours, suppression and mutual exclusion: a user in nurture is ineligible for win-back; a user in trial is ineligible for nurture; win-back cannot fire on any account under 30 days old.
  7. Every email carries the anti-impersonation line.

Consent capture at the gate: two unticked, separately-worded boxes (§4.6). Box 1 = contract performance (carries no offer if box 2 is unticked); box 2 = marketing (GDPR 6(1)(a) + PECR 22). SMS would be a third box. Expected cost: box 2 will be ticked by materially fewer people. That is the point — the unticked share are the people who would have complained.

9.4 ⚠️ The legal boundary — where a fleet stops being architecture

Meta Account Integrity (rev. 2026-05-28) allows disabling assets "Owned by the same person or entity as an account that has been disabled" and "Created or repurposed to evade a previous account or entity removal, including those assessed to have common ownership and content", plus "close linkage" / "coordination within a network". Also enumerated: "Creating or using an account… through automated means, such as scripting." And the Ad Standards separately prohibit "sell, rent, buy or exchange site privileges" ⇒ buying an aged Business Manager or agency account is a named violation. We are not doing it.

A fleet built BEFORE a ban is redundancy. A rebuild AFTER one is an enumerated takedown trigger, and liability propagates to surviving assets.

  1. Second-person diagnosis of a personal attribute. The highest-frequency violation shape in the corpus and the one our best hooks naturally drift into (§6.5). Mitigation: object-first framing, already applied to all 18 concepts.

Not on this list, deliberately: "astrology is banned" (it is not — astrology/psychic is NOT a restricted category and there is no permission to apply for), "you need permission for spiritual ads", "quiz funnels are prohibited" (2,315 ads say otherwise).

9.7 Scaling — the one piece of arithmetic that matters

Meta's learning phase requires "about 50 results in the week after the ad set's last significant edit", and "editing an ad, ad set or campaign during the learning phase… resets learning."

weekly budget floor (per ad set) = 50 × (your CPA for the optimised event)

We have no CPA for this vertical and will not invent one. The operational consequences are what matter:

  • Optimise for the event you can actually get ~50 of per week. At launch that is LEAD (email capture), not PURCHASE. Purchase-optimisation on a fresh pixel with a $1 front end is the classic way to sit in "Learning limited" forever.
  • Two ad sets, not six — every additional ad set multiplies the 50-event floor.
  • Move to Purchase only once the dataset supplies 50 purchases/week, then layer value-based bidding.

The sequence:

  • Phase 0, before any spend (one sitting): both portfolios + 2FA · buy funnel domain and cold spare, verify both by DNS TXT in Portfolio A · create all four Pages with full profiles + 3–6 posts each · dataset + ?pid= router + external_id + CAPI on ev. with event_id dedup · ad-link editing permissions restricted to our Pages · both payment cards added and one small confirmed payment made · phone-verify if prompted · QA the funnel against §9.6 — this is a gate, not a checklist item.
  • Phase 1, days 1–7: AA1 only, P1 only, optimise Lead, broad targeting (no interest stacks — which also removes a whole class of targeting-review risk), 4–6 creatives in one ad set, spend near (not over) the Meta-set daily limit, and do not edit anything for 7 days.
  • Phase 2, days 8–21: add P2 (pick-a-card) in AA1 · create AA2 for Tier B at a small budget, to have history · raise AA1 budget in steps of ≤20–30% and never while an ad set is in learning (a practitioner heuristic, not a Meta-published number — its only defensible justification is "avoid a learning reset") · first creative refresh, keep the ad set.
  • Phase 3, days 22–45: P3 live, AA3 created and small · switch to Purchase once ≥50/week · start AA4 at $5–10/day on a compliant awareness campaign so it is warm · P4 stays dark, accumulating age.
  • Phase 4, day 45+: scale by budget on the existing ad set (preserving learning) before adding new ad sets; watch the per-Page ad-slot ceiling (Meta warns at 20% of the limit).

The three things most likely to go wrong, in order: (1) learning-limited at $1-CPA-equivalent volumes (fix: consolidate, optimise Lead); (2) hitting the daily spending limit and misreading it as a performance problem (fix: check Payment settings, spend near the limit so it lifts); (3) a rejection cascade from one landing-page element (fix: the §9.6 gate, done before launch).

  • US federal: ROSCA is the binding law and it never went away. Civil penalty $53,088/violation.
  • The FTC "click-to-cancel" rule was VACATED in full by the 8th Circuit on 8 Jul 2025. ANPRM 11 Mar 2026, comments closed 13 Apr 2026, no NPRM as of today (searched). ROSCA + §5 enforcement never paused — see §10.1. Build to the vacated rule anyway.
  • ⭐ The US state ARL patchwork is the binding constraint, not ROSCA: CA (1 Jul 2025) · AR (3 Aug 2025) · CO (6 Aug 2025) · MA (2 Sep 2025) · NY (5 Nov 2025) · ME (1 Jan 2026) · MD (1 Jun 2026) · CT (1 Jul 2026). Build ONE cancel flow that satisfies CA + CO + CT and every state is covered: one-click, one-step, immediately effective; save offers BESIDE the live cancel button, never before it; reason asked AFTER cancellation and skippable.
  • EU/IE: CRD Art. 8(2) (the pay button, D12) · UCPD Annex I No. 7 (false limited-time) and 23b/23c (reviews) are per-se bans with no balancing · CCRs reg. 40 / CRD Art. 22 (no pre-ticking) · CCRs reg. 37 — instantly-delivered digital add-ons need express consent and acknowledgement ("I understand I lose my 14-day right to cancel") before generation, or we must refund and cannot recover the content.
